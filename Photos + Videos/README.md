## üõ†Ô∏è Engineering Methodology

### 1. Mechanical Design & Fabrication
The chassis was fabricated entirely in-house using precise metal cutting, grinding, and welding. 
* **Structural Analysis:** The frame was designed to support a gross weight of 8 adults plus the 100Ah battery pack.
* **Ergonomics:** Custom carpentry was applied to the seating and bodywork to balance weight and passenger comfort.



### 2. Electrical Powertrain & Solar Integration
* **Efficiency:** The MPPT controller ensures that the Li-ion batteries receive the maximum possible wattage from the solar canopy.
* **Performance:** The 3kW motor provides high torque, essential for maintaining speed with a full passenger load.

### 3. Additive Manufacturing (DfAM)
Using **Autodesk Fusion 360**, custom parts were modeled and printed to ensure a professional "OEM" fit and finish for the dashboard and control interfaces.

---

## üìà Testing & Validation
The vehicle underwent a multi-stage validation process:
1. **Static Load Testing:** Verifying chassis deflection under max passenger weight.
2. **Charging Efficiency:** Measuring solar harvest rates in various weather conditions.
3. **Dynamic Performance:** Testing braking distance and turning radius for campus safety standards.
